Onboarding Steps

Follow these steps to connect your website to ProCyberWall protection. Each step is described in full — bookmark this page and work through it at your own pace.

✓

Step 1 — Account Created

Your ProCyberWall account is active. You are logged in and can access this dashboard. No further action is needed for this step.

2

Step 2 — Share Your Domain With Us

WhatsApp us your website domain (e.g. shopkaro.in) so we can register it in our system and assign Cloudflare firewall rules.

Tap the button below to open WhatsApp — just send your domain URL in the message.

💬 WhatsApp Us
3

Step 3 — Update Your Nameservers

This step routes your domain's DNS through Cloudflare so ProCyberWall can inspect and filter all traffic before it reaches your website.

Your ProCyberWall nameservers are:

NS 1
adi.ns.cloudflare.com
NS 2
newt.ns.cloudflare.com

How to update nameservers (step-by-step):

  1. Log in to the registrar where you bought your domain — e.g. GoDaddy, Namecheap, BigRock, Google Domains, or Hostinger.
  2. Navigate to Domain Management → find your domain → click Manage or DNS Settings.
  3. Look for a section labelled Nameservers or Custom Nameservers.
  4. Select "Custom" or "Use my own nameservers" (instead of the default registrar nameservers).
  5. Delete the existing nameserver entries and enter the two values above:
    adi.ns.cloudflare.com
    newt.ns.cloudflare.com
  6. Save / confirm the changes.

The whole process takes about 2 minutes. If you get stuck, WhatsApp us and we'll walk you through it live.

4

Step 4 — Protection Goes Live

After you update your nameservers, DNS propagation across the internet takes anywhere from 10 minutes to 24 hours. This is normal and outside anyone's control.

Once propagation is complete:

  • Your firewall rules are activated automatically.
  • Live threat data will appear on your Overview dashboard.
  • We will send you a WhatsApp confirmation message the moment everything is live.

You do not need to do anything else. Sit back — we handle the rest.

What to do if your emails are exposed
1️⃣
Change the password for every affected email account immediately. Use a unique password you haven't used anywhere else.
2️⃣
Enable 2-factor authentication on all email accounts and any service that uses those emails to log in.
3️⃣
Check for suspicious activity — look for emails you didn't send, accounts you didn't create, or logins from unknown devices.
4️⃣
Contact ProCyberWall support if you need help — we can assist with DMARC setup and email hardening to prevent future exposure.
